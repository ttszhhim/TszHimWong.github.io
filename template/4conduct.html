Reflection on Professional Conduct

Understanding Professionalism

This module helped me develop a clearer understanding of what professionalism means both as a student and a future technology professional. According to the BCS Code of Conduct, professionalism includes upholding integrity, acting in the public interest, maintaining competence, and showing respect for others.

Examples of My Conduct

Throughout the module, I demonstrated professionalism by:

  • Consistently attending workshops on time
  • Submitting tasks before deadlines
  • Respecting tutors and peers during discussions
  • Using clear and polite communication in emails and written tasks

Areas for Improvement

While I have demonstrated basic professionalism, there are areas where I can continue to improve:

  • Engagement: I tend to stay quiet in group settingsâ€”I want to contribute more actively.
  • Confidence: I sometimes hesitate to share my technical ideas.
  • Attention to detail: I need to proofread written work more carefully before submission.

Development Plan

To improve further, I will:

  • Practice speaking in group tasks and online forums
  • Review the BCS Code regularly and apply it when writing reports and communicating with others
  • Seek feedback from tutors and peers to enhance my professionalism

By addressing these areas, I aim to grow into a more responsible, respectful, and competent computing professional ready to contribute ethically to future workplaces.
